About Me

Shivam Gupta is a PhD candidate in the Department of Physics at IIT Kanpur, working under Prof. Krishnacharya in the Soft Matter Physics Laboratory. His thesis, "Liquids on Lubricated Surfaces: Statics and Dynamics," examines what happens when a drop of one liquid lands on a solid surface pre-coated with a thin lubricating film — systems inspired by the slippery, insect-trapping surface of the Nepenthes pitcher plant.

His work sits at the intersection of experiment and simulation: measuring nanometre-scale lubricant films with interferometry and confocal microscopy, and modelling the resulting interfacial shapes with Surface Evolver and custom numerical solvers.

Research Details

Exploring the morphology of lubricant-coated slippery systems when an external drop comes into contact with them — the equilibrium shapes they settle into, and the film dynamics along the way.

Studies combine experiment and numerics to resolve both the quasi-equilibrium interfacial profile and the transient dynamics of the underlying lubricant film:

  • Interfacial tension & contact angle measurement
  • Chemical modification via grafting and photolithography
  • Optical, fluorescence & dual-wavelength RICM
  • Numerical solutions of governing PDEs
  • Quasi-static interfacial profile via Surface Evolver

In Motion

Drop impact, film dewetting, and lubricant flow — captured through microscopes and interferometry rigs.
